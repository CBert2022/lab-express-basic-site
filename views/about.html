Sir David Attenborough

Sir David Frederick Attenborough was born on May 8, 1926. He is an English broadcaster and naturalist.

He is best known for writing and presenting the nine natural history documentary series that form the Life collection. He is also a former senior manager at the BBC, having served as controller of BBC Two and director of programming for BBC Television in the 1960s and 1970s.

Attenborough is widely considered a national treasure in Britain. He has won numerous awards during his career, including three BAFTAs, two Golden Globes, a Peabody Award, and an Emmy Award.
